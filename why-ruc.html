Road funding basics

GAS TAX:

Our current funding source for roads and bridges

The construction and maintenance of highways is currently funded through a state gas tax of 49.4 cents per gallon that drivers pay at the pump.

THE FUNDING SOURCE WILL RUN OUT

Washington State law has set forth the goal of no more gas-powered cars by 2030.

As more of the cars on our roads are electric or more fuel-efficient, funding from the gas tax is expected to decline. In order to generate current levels of revenue, the gas tax would need to increase 1.7 cents per gallon per year. This would mean a gas tax of over 90 cents per gallon by 2040.

Why RUC?

Charging by the mile, rather than by the gallon of gas or an annual vehicle registration fee, harmonizes transportation funding with climate policy objectives and efforts to bring greater equity to transportation taxation.

A RUC establishes a user-pay, user-benefit funding approach, giving all drivers a way to contribute based upon how much we use the roads.

Importantly–RUC will not be an additional tax to drivers!
